No more worrying about the price of gas.

Significantly lower maintenance costs. Credits and incentives against purchase or lease, plus other discounts and special rates. When you do the math, the lifetime savings on an electric vehicle are more than worth it.

Electricity averages one-third to one-fourth the cost of gasoline on a per-mile basis. On top of that, electric vehicles don't require oil changes, replacing filters or spark plugs, are often exempt from emissions tests, and require less brake maintenance. Several studies have added it all up and found that EV owners save roughly $10,000 over five years compared to traditional gas vehicle owners.

content-block-1 right

Today's electric vehicles are leading the way when it comes to high-powered driving performance. Ask electric vehicle owners what it's like to drive their car and you'll see.

As members of the rEVolution, they have discovered the benefits of a motor that doesn't require revving or shifting gears to create speed. They're enjoying the instant torque and responsiveness that you get without so much heavy internal machinery. They know they can compete with — or beat — traditional cars in going from 0-60 mph.

Performance doesn't only matter to gearheads or sports car enthusiasts, though. All drivers appreciate the convenience of quickly passing that one slow car in traffic, or the reassurance of responsive handling on a tight sharp turn.

Electric motors can handle whatever the road throws at them, and they do it safely and efficiently. And that's appealing to everyone.

More Ways You Save with EVs

The fuel cost and lifetime maintenance advantage are just the beginning of your savings as an EV owner. You'll also enjoy:

  • Federal tax credits plus state incentives
  • Special rates from your energy company, especially for off-peak charging at home
  • Luxury feel and complimentary higher-end options that you don't get with cheaper gas models
  • Free access to many carpool or HOV lanes
  • Free parking and/or charging at some public locations
  • Registration and property tax exemptions in many states
  • No more worrying about fluctuations in gas prices
  • Reduced monthly lease payments or discounted/special loan terms
  • Lower insurance rates in many states
